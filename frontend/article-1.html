What Is Stelluna? A Professional Guide to Stelluna Bodhi

13 Mar 2025

Stelluna Bodhi bead, is a natural seed used to create spiritual and decorative bracelets and necklaces. With its calming appearance and deep cultural roots, Stelluna is not just jewelry — it's a symbol of nature's beauty, spiritual connection, and artisan tradition.


A Gift from Nature

Stelluna originates from the fruit of the rattan palm tree — a climbing plant in the Arecaceae (palm) family, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The fruit is called rattan fruit, and it holds the raw seed that becomes Stelluna after careful processing.

From Raw Seed to Stelluna Bead

The transformation from raw rattan seed to smooth, elegant Stelluna bead involves careful handcrafting — sanding, shaping, and polishing each piece by hand. No two beads are exactly alike, and each carries its own subtle pattern and texture gifted by nature itself.

The result is something truly unique: a bead that combines the natural wild, organic spirit with the quiet refinement of artisan hands.

The outer skin of the rattan fruit has a scale-like texture, similar to a snake fruit (salak). Inside, the structure is somewhat like a longan — a large seed surrounded by thin, astringent flesh. Though technically edible, the fruit is not commonly eaten due to its strong, bitter taste.

The core seed, however, is where the magic happens. Once harvested, these seeds are picked, dried, polished, and hand-shaped into the iconic beads. The natural contrast of creamy surfaces dotted with dark spots gives rise to the name "Star-and-Moon" — the bud points with the blanks around representing moon and the dots representing the stars.

More Than Just a Bead

Worn for spiritual meditation, fashion, or cultural appreciation, Stelluna bracelets and necklaces offer a connection — not just to nature, but to something deeper. Over time, the beads change color, developing a warm amber tone and reflecting the life and energy of the person who wears them.

Whether you're discovering Stelluna for the first time or already drawn to its mystique, this small, natural bead holds a world of beauty, tradition, and story within.
